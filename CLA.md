## How to sign

PxO accepts CLA sign-off using the **Developer Certificate of Origin (DCO)** style sign-off plus an explicit one-time CLA acceptance comment on your first pull request. There are two paths:

### A. Individual contributors

1. **Sign your commits** with `git commit -s` (or `--signoff`). This appends a line of the form `Signed-off-by: Your Name <you@example.com>` to every commit message.
2. **On your first pull request**, leave a comment containing exactly:

   > I have read the PxO CLA at `CLA.md` and I accept its terms for all my past, present, and future Contributions to PxO.
   > Signed: \<Your Full Legal Name\>, \<your@email\>, \<YYYY-MM-DD\>

3. The Project Owner will record your acceptance in the project's contributor ledger before your PR is merged.

### B. Corporate contributors

If you are contributing on behalf of an employer, or your employer has rights in your work, **also** email a Corporate CLA acknowledgement to <mark@paradoxrooms.com> before your first PR. The acknowledgement should state:

- Company legal name and address.
- An authorized signatory (name, title, email).
- A list of GitHub usernames authorized to contribute on the company's behalf.
- A statement that the company agrees to the terms of this CLA for those contributors' Contributions.

The Project Owner will reply to confirm acceptance and update the contributor ledger.

## Why a CLA?

PxO is dual-licensed: AGPL-3.0 by default, with a separate commercial license available for closed-source / proprietary deployments (see [COMMERCIAL.md](COMMERCIAL.md)). The commercial license model only works if the Project Owner has the right to relicense all the code in the project. The CLA is what makes that right explicit — without it, every contributor would retain veto power over commercial relicensing of their code, which would break the dual-license model.

This CLA is modeled on the widely used Apache Individual CLA and the Harmony project CLA, adapted to PxO's dual-license structure. If you have legal questions before signing, please raise an issue or email <mark@paradoxrooms.com>.
