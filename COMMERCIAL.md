## Commercial license

If any of the following apply, you need a separate **commercial license** from the copyright holder:

1. You want to embed PxO (or a derivative work) inside a closed-source product, service, or appliance.
2. You want to distribute PxO (or a derivative work) under terms other than AGPL-3.0.
3. You want to offer PxO-derived functionality as part of a commercial product or paid service without releasing your full source under AGPL-3.0.
4. Your organization's policies prohibit AGPL-licensed software.

The commercial license is negotiated on a case-by-case basis. Typical structures:

- One-time per-deployment fee.
- Per-installation royalty.
- Annual revenue share.
- OEM redistribution agreement.

## Contributor licensing

To preserve the ability to offer commercial licenses, all outside contributors must agree to the PxO **Contributor License Agreement** before their contributions are merged. The CLA grants the Project Owner a perpetual license to relicense Contributions under any license, including proprietary commercial licenses — which is what makes the dual-license model work.

See [CLA.md](CLA.md) for the full agreement text and the sign-off process for individual and corporate contributors.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I use PxO for free in my escape room?**
A: Yes, under AGPL-3.0, as long as you comply with its terms (notably, sharing source for any modifications you deploy).

**Q: Can I bundle PxO inside a hardware product I sell?**
A: Only under AGPL-3.0 (which would force your bundled software stack to also be AGPL-licensed and source-available) or under a commercial license. Most commercial vendors choose the commercial license.

**Q: Can I fork PxO on GitHub?**
A: Yes. Forks are governed by AGPL-3.0 unless you obtain a commercial license.
